DOCUMENT: Personal Finance Fundamentals
SOURCE: TriDomain Finance KB v1.0

Financial stability begins with understanding income, expenses, savings, and investments.
The recommended budgeting rule is the 50-30-20 rule:
- 50% for essential needs (rent, groceries, utilities)
- 30% for personal wants (entertainment, travel)
- 20% for savings and investments

Track expenses every month to identify unnecessary spending.
Maintaining financial discipline is more important than earning a high salary.

DOCUMENT: Emergency Fund Guide
SOURCE: TriDomain Finance KB v1.0

An emergency fund protects against unexpected financial situations.

Recommended emergency fund:
- Students: 3 months of expenses
- Salaried employees: 6 months of expenses
- Freelancers or business owners: 9–12 months of expenses

Emergency funds should be kept in highly liquid accounts such as savings accounts or liquid mutual funds.

Never invest your emergency fund in high-risk assets.

DOCUMENT: Saving Strategies
SOURCE: TriDomain Finance KB v1.0

Effective saving habits include:
1. Pay yourself first before spending.
2. Automate monthly savings.
3. Avoid impulse purchases.
4. Track recurring subscriptions.
5. Increase savings whenever income increases.

Saving consistently is more effective than saving irregular large amounts.

DOCUMENT: Financial Ratios
SOURCE: TriDomain Finance KB v1.0

Important personal finance ratios:

Savings Rate =
Monthly Savings / Monthly Income × 100

Expense Ratio =
Monthly Expenses / Monthly Income × 100

Debt-to-Income Ratio =
Monthly EMI / Monthly Income × 100

Recommended values:
Savings Rate >20%
Expense Ratio <80%
Debt-to-Income <35%
