DOCUMENT: Career Transition Guide
SOURCE: TriDomain Career KB v1.0

Switching careers to data science from a non-technical background is achievable in 6-18 months.
The recommended learning path for career transition:
Phase 1: Python fundamentals — 4 weeks — Kaggle Python course is free and highly rated
Phase 2: Statistics and mathematics — 4 weeks — Khan Academy Statistics is recommended
Phase 3: Machine learning fundamentals — 6 weeks — Andrew Ng Coursera course is industry standard
Phase 4: SQL and databases — 2 weeks — Mode Analytics SQL tutorial is free
Phase 5: Portfolio projects — 4 weeks — Build 2-3 projects on Kaggle competitions
Phase 6: Job preparation — 2 weeks — Resume, LinkedIn, mock interviews

Never quit your current job before completing at least Phase 3 and having 2 portfolio projects.
Financial safety net of 6 months expenses is recommended before full transition.

DOCUMENT: Resume and Portfolio Guide
SOURCE: TriDomain Career KB v1.0

A strong data science resume should include:
- Quantified achievements: "Improved model accuracy by 15%" not "worked on ML models"
- GitHub portfolio with at least 2-3 projects with README documentation
- Kaggle profile showing competition participation and notebooks
- LinkedIn profile with skills section matching job descriptions
- Online certifications from Coursera, edX, or Google are valued by Indian employers

Top companies hiring data scientists in India:
Flipkart, Amazon, Google, Microsoft, Walmart Labs, PhonePe, Swiggy, Zomato, Razorpay, CRED

DOCUMENT: Salary Negotiation Guide
SOURCE: TriDomain Career KB v1.0

Salary negotiation tips for Indian job market:
1. Always research market rates on Glassdoor, AmbitionBox, and Levels.fyi before negotiating
2. 80% of job offers in India have room for 10-20% negotiation
3. Never reveal your current salary if possible — focus on market rate
4. Ask for time to consider offers — never accept on the spot
5. Consider total compensation: base salary, variable pay, ESOPs, health insurance
6. For experienced professionals, job switching typically yields 30-50% salary increase
7. Internal promotions in India average only 10-15% increase
8. The best time to switch jobs is 2-3 years into a role

DOCUMENT: Skill Gap Analysis Framework
SOURCE: TriDomain Career KB v1.0

How to close skill gaps efficiently:
- Focus on the top 3 missing skills first — do not try to learn everything at once
- Dedicate minimum 2 hours per day for consistent progress
- Project-based learning is 3x more effective than theory alone
- Join communities: Kaggle, GitHub, local Python meetups in Bangalore
- Mock interviews on platforms like Pramp and InterviewBit improve success rate by 40%
- Getting a mentor accelerates learning by 60% according to industry studies

Common mistakes during career transition:
1. Learning too many tools without mastering any
2. Not building a portfolio while learning
3. Waiting until "ready" to apply — start applying at 70% readiness
4. Ignoring soft skills — communication is rated highly by Indian tech companies
5. Not networking — 60% of jobs in India are filled through referrals

DOCUMENT: Work Life Balance and Career Stress
SOURCE: TriDomain Career KB v1.0

Managing stress during career transition:
- Career transitions are stressful — this is normal and temporary
- Set realistic timelines — rushing increases anxiety and reduces learning quality
- Maintain current job performance during transition to avoid termination
- Talk to your manager about growth opportunities before deciding to switch
- Burnout is a real risk — schedule regular breaks and maintain hobbies
- If job stress is severely affecting health, consult a professional counselor
- Never make major career decisions during periods of extreme stress
- Financial stress compounds career stress — maintain emergency fund of 6 months
